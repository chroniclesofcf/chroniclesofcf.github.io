Vettel out? Alonso to return? Silly season begins

This season of the Formula 1 travel circus hasn't got off to a great start.
What is usually a specatacle of 10 teams and 20 drivers battling it out on track
for supremecy has been diluted to empty grand stands and terrible business decisions.
It's not all dark clouds and flat tires though as the driver market is about to spice
up this drought of exciting races we've endured. With many of the drivers contracts
running out at the end of this season, we expect to see some movement in the driver market,
possibly even some wildcards.

As early as Australia last year, the rumour mills been churning stories of Sebastian Vettel's contract and Lewis Hamilton on a possible move to Ferrari. News broke earlier this year that Vettel had been offered a one-year extensions but at a substantial pay cut. News broke recently of Vettel declining the contract from Ferrari branding it a "joke" at £12m for the year. A far cry from his usual 3 year contracts at £40m anually. There's many reasons Vettel could have refused to sign the contract and looking into it begins to reveal the cracks reminiscent of his last year at Red Bull.

Ferrari have began to make a short list of drivers suited to replace Vettel in the case they can't come to an agreement on a contract that suits them both. On the list are names like Daniel Ricciardo, Carlos Sainz and Antonio Giovinazzi. According to F1-Insider.com, two time world champion and ex Ferrari driver, Fernando Alonso, has even thrown his hat into the ring, putting himself forward as a replacement for Vettel.

With the season on hold till later this year, now is the time for negotiations to happen.
Their be plenty more news around contracts but take everything with a bucket of salt until
it all comes to fruition.
